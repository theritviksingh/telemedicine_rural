üåç Why Nabha? Our Research-Based Problem Statement

Understanding the specific healthcare challenges facing Nabha and its surrounding rural communities

Nabha, a semi-urban town in Patiala district, Punjab, is surrounded by dozens of rural villages with limited healthcare infrastructure. Despite having a population of over 100,000 in the region, access to specialists, diagnostics, and pharmacies remains scarce.

üîç Key Issues Identified Through Research

üöó

Distance & Transport Barriers

Residents often travel 20‚Äì50 km to reach secondary hospitals, making healthcare costly and time-consuming.

üèóÔ∏è

Infrastructure Gaps

Primary health centres lack diagnostic labs, equipment, and specialist doctors. Many patients rely on occasional health camps.

‚è∞

Delayed Care

Low awareness and lack of early diagnostic support lead to late-stage detection of chronic diseases like diabetes, hypertension, and respiratory illnesses.

üó®Ô∏è

Language & Literacy Challenges

Many patients speak only Punjabi and struggle with technical or English medical terms, creating communication barriers.

üí∞

Economic Strain

Treatment costs, travel expenses, and loss of daily wages discourage timely care, especially for daily-wage workers and farmers.

‚õ∫

Reliance on Health Camps

Patients depend on occasional health camps (e.g., free eye camps for cataract treatment) rather than consistent healthcare access.
